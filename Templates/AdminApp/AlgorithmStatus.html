Project Overview

To enhance security and privacy in connected vehicles against **Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)**, machine learning algorithms such as **XGBoost** (Extreme Gradient Boosting), **Random Forest (RF)**, and **Decision Trees (DT)** play a significant role when integrated with **Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)** techniques.

These algorithms are employed to detect anomalies and suspicious behaviors in vehicle networks by learning patterns from network traffic, sensor data, and system logs. **Decision Trees (DT)** are simple yet powerful models that make decisions based on interpretable rules. **Random Forest (RF)**, an ensemble of decision trees, offers better accuracy and robustness. **XGBoost**, a highly efficient gradient boosting algorithm, provides even higher accuracy and is particularly effective in detecting sophisticated APTs due to its ability to capture complex non-linear relationships and feature interactions.
